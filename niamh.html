The Grand Innovation Challenge Easter 2019
This was a university project aimed at identifying and resolving a problem presented by electric cars in modern society. By using an innovation framework, I collaborated within an interdisciplinary team, to devise a work-based car lending scheme designed to market electric cars to the professional worker.
I learnt how to work in a team and how to leverage team capabilities to create a successful project. I led the product pitch at the end of the challenge which developed my presentation skills and highlighted the importance of team communication in creating a successful launch.
Ping Pong Ball Launcher Project, University of Bath Spring-Autumn 2019
The brief given, was to design a mechanically operated ping pong ball launcher that launched three balls different specified lengths, as part of a group of 5. Through this, I utilised different concept generation and selection methods to design a working model which we then manufactured in the following semester.
Through this project I developed my report writing skills and improved my CAD skills. I paid particular attention to my part and assembly drawings which I then used to assemble the project in the second semester.
